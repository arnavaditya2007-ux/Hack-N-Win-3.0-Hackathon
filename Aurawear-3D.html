PPG Optical Engine

Tracking Physics: Emits monochromatic green light at 525nm. As the heart beats, blood volume in the finger capillaries changes, modulating the light's reflection. A high-speed photodiod array captures these micro-oscillations at 50 samples per second.

Signal Processing: The onboard M4 processor applies a recursive Kalman Filter to strip motion artifacts and ambient light noise, isolating the raw plethysmogram wave.

Data Management: Filtered R-R intervals are compressed using lossless delta-encoding and saved to an encrypted NOR flash sector. Data is stored locally for up to 30 days if not synced.

Pulse Oximetry Array

Dual-Wavelength Physics: Alternates between 660nm (Red) and 940nm (Infrared) emitters. Oxygenated hemoglobin absorbs more IR light, while deoxygenated hemoglobin absorbs more red light. The ring calculates the "Ratio of Ratios" (R) to determine peripheral oxygen saturation (SpO2).

Temporal Saving: To optimize battery, SpO2 logic executes in 30-second bursts every 5 minutes during sleep. Raw logs are converted to a compressed bit-stream before being saved into the secure system sector.

Protection: All SpO2 histograms are locked behind 256-bit hardware encryption on the ring's internal HSM module.

🌡️

MEMS Thermopile

Direct IR Sensing: Unlike traditional NTC thermistors, AuraWear uses a non-contact MEMS thermopile that detects long-wave IR radiation. This allows for absolute skin temperature tracking with 0.05°C precision, unaffected by air gaps between the ring and finger.

Baseline Creation: The firmware aggregates hourly readings to create a "Basal Body Temperature" (BBT) baseline. This baseline is saved locally and updated every 24 hours to track metabolic and cycle variances.

Encrypted Logs: Every thermal data point is timestamped and signed with a unique hardware key (UID), ensuring that your metabolic history is never stored as plain text.

Bio-Impedance Array

Cellular Tracking: Sends a sub-threshold alternating current across the finger. By measuring the electrical resistance (impedance) and reactance, the system calculates the ratio of extracellular to intracellular water.

Data Results: This is used to derive body composition (fat vs. lean mass) and hydration levels. The raw impedance data is never exported; only the calculated "composition vectors" are saved to the secure user partition.

Lifecycle: Analysis is triggered during specific stationary windows during the day to ensure maximum accuracy. Results are stored in a rolling 7-day circular buffer on the device.
